Q1 / 05

Who should come off the list?

13 hard bounces  +  7 repeat soft-bounce threads  +  37 zero-open addresses  =  39 unique removals

Hard bounces mean the address is dead — it will never deliver, so those 13 come out immediately. Seven more recipients soft-bounced on two back-to-back sends, which usually signals a permanently full or abandoned inbox. And 37 recipients never opened a single one of the 16 emails across the full two months — they're dragging down deliverability and sender reputation without any upside. Removing the list gets Snappos down to 211 active recipients.

Afternoon sends lead on both open rate and click rate, with evening close behind. Snappos customers are shopping for themselves, not for work — lunchtime and post-workday are when personal inboxes actually get attention, while early-morning inboxes are dominated by work email and people rushing out the door.

Both open rate and click rate follow the same pattern for women and men: engagement is far higher when the section-1 product matches the recipient's gender than when it doesn't. Sending men's promotions to women (or vice versa) wastes sends on content people are unlikely to act on — splitting into a Men's segment and a Women's segment would lift engagement at no extra cost.

Q5 / 05

Who belongs in a re-engagement campaign?

44 recipients opened in the first month, then went silent

These 44 proved they'll open a Snappos email at least once, so their recent silence looks more like fatigue or a full inbox than a dead address — unlike the 39 recommended for removal above. A "we miss you" send with a stronger incentive, or a preference-update request, is more likely to revive them than continuing business as usual, and it protects open rates and sender reputation better than repeatedly emailing an unresponsive segment.
